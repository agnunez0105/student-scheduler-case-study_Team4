Booked & Busy

Helping students balance academics, clubs, and life

Overview Problem Role Process Outcome Reflection

Project Overview

Booked & Busy is a scheduling app designed to help college students manage their time between classes, study sessions, extracurriculars, and social activities.

This was a group UX project completed by Arianna Nunez, Cecilia Bilski, and Alexander Clarke.

The app was inspired by real student struggles with burnout, time conflicts, and difficulty balancing academic responsibilities with campus involvement.

Problem

Students often struggle to balance academic responsibilities with extracurricular involvement, leading to stress, burnout, and poor time management.

  • Conflicting schedules between classes and clubs
  • Lack of structured planning tools
  • Overwhelming workload and poor time visibility
  • Difficulty making decisions under pressure

From our research, we identified major themes including academic overload, mental strain, sleep disruption, and awareness gaps around healthy habits.

Role

I contributed to research, persona development, and early design concepts for the app.

  • Participated in user research and interviews
  • Helped build affinity diagrams
  • Developed the persona “Ella”
  • Contributed to design ideas and feature planning
  • Collaborated with team members on prototype direction

Process

Research

We conducted interviews and used affinity mapping to identify patterns in student experiences related to stress, workload, and time management.

Persona

We created a persona named Ella, who represents students trying to balance academics and social involvement.

  • Goal: Stay involved while maintaining grades
  • Pain point: Constant time conflicts
  • Emotional state: Overwhelmed and stressed

Design Ideas

  • Calendar integration
  • Daily schedule view
  • Automatic study-time insertion
  • Club discovery and selection
  • Social schedule sharing

Prototype

The prototype focused on onboarding, building a schedule, adding clubs, and automatically inserting study time before activities.

Testing

We tested the prototype with students and gathered feedback on usability and clarity.

  • Users liked schedule integration
  • Confusion around permissions and editing features
  • Needed clearer feedback for actions

Outcome

The final concept was a smart scheduling tool that helps students plan their time more effectively and reduce stress.

  • Combines academic and social scheduling
  • Automatically suggests study time
  • Improves decision-making
  • Encourages balance and structure

The project demonstrated how design can help reduce uncertainty and support better time management for students.

Reflection

My biggest challenge was organizing a large amount of research into a clear and structured case study. There were many components, including research, personas, sketches, and testing.

Next, I plan to improve the visual design of the site and include images from the prototype and research process.

I would like feedback on whether the case study clearly connects research to design decisions and if the overall flow is easy to understand.
