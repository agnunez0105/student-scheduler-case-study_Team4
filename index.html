Project Overview

Booked & Busy is a UX group project focused on helping college students manage their time between classes, study sessions, extracurriculars, and social life.

This project was completed by Arianna Nunez, Cecilia Bilski, and Alexander Clarke. It was inspired by real student struggles with burnout, time conflicts, and decision-making under pressure.

Problem

Students often struggle to balance academic responsibilities with extracurricular involvement. This can lead to stress, burnout, poor time management, and difficulty deciding what to prioritize.

  • Conflicting schedules between classes, study time, and clubs
  • Lack of structured planning tools
  • Overwhelming workload during busy weeks
  • Difficulty making decisions under pressure
Design Goal: Help students manage their time in a way that supports both productivity and campus involvement without adding more stress.

Role

My role included contributing to research, persona development, design planning, and early prototype direction.

  • Participated in research and interview planning
  • Helped build and organize affinity diagrams
  • Contributed to the persona “Ella”
  • Helped connect research findings to design features
  • Worked with my team on prototype direction and case study development

Process

Research & Affinity Mapping

We analyzed student experiences and organized insights using affinity diagrams. This helped us identify patterns related to stress, workload, sleep, burnout, and scheduling challenges.

Persona Development

We created a persona named Ella, who represents students trying to balance academics and social involvement. Ella helped us focus the design on reducing uncertainty and supporting better planning.

Design Ideas

Based on our research, we focused on features that would help students see their full schedule, protect study time, and still stay connected to clubs and campus activities.

  • Calendar integration
  • Daily schedule view
  • Automatic study-time insertion
  • Club selection and confirmation
  • Social schedule sharing

Prototype

The prototype demonstrates onboarding, schedule creation, club selection, and automated study planning before activities.

Outcome

The final concept is a scheduling tool that helps students plan their time more effectively while reducing stress and improving decision-making.

  • Combines academic and social scheduling in one place
  • Automatically suggests study time before activities
  • Improves time visibility
  • Supports healthier balance between school and campus involvement

Reflection

My biggest challenge was organizing a large amount of research, diagrams, sketches, and prototype work into one clear case study. I focused on making the story easy to follow from problem to process to outcome.

What are you planning to improve or polish next?

What feedback would you like?

I would like feedback on whether the case study clearly explains how our research influenced the app features and whether the overall site is easy to follow.
