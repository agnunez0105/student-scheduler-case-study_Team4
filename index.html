Booked & Busy

A scheduling app designed to help students balance academics, study time, and campus involvement.

Overview Problem Role Process Outcome Reflection Team

Project Overview

Booked & Busy is a UX group project focused on helping college students manage their time between classes, study sessions, extracurriculars, and social life.

This project was completed by Arianna Nunez, Cecilia Bilski, and Alexander Clarke. It was inspired by real student struggles with burnout, time conflicts, and decision-making under pressure.

Problem

Students struggle to balance academic responsibilities with extracurricular involvement, leading to stress, burnout, and poor time management.

  • Conflicting schedules between classes and clubs
  • Lack of structured planning tools
  • Overwhelming workload
  • Difficulty making decisions under pressure
Design Goal: Help students manage their time in a way that supports both productivity and involvement without burnout.

Role

I contributed to research, persona development, and early design concepts.

  • Participated in research and interviews
  • Helped build affinity diagrams
  • Developed the persona “Ella”
  • Contributed to feature planning
  • Worked with team members on prototype direction

Process

Research & Affinity Mapping

We analyzed student experiences and organized insights using affinity diagrams to identify patterns related to stress, workload, and scheduling challenges.

Persona Development

We created a persona named Ella, representing students trying to balance academics and social involvement.

Design Ideas

  • Calendar integration
  • Daily schedule view
  • Automatic study-time insertion
  • Club selection and confirmation
  • Social schedule sharing

Prototype

The prototype demonstrates onboarding, schedule creation, and automated study planning before activities.

Outcome

The final concept is a scheduling tool that helps students plan their time more effectively while reducing stress and improving decision-making.

  • Combines academic and social scheduling
  • Automatically suggests study time
  • Improves time visibility
  • Supports better balance

Reflection

My biggest challenge was organizing a large amount of research into a clear and structured case study.

Next, I plan to improve the visual design and include more polished prototype visuals.

I would like feedback on whether the connection between research and design decisions is clear and easy to follow.
